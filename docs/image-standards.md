## Overview

Consistent product images are critical for a professional storefront.
Inconsistent image sizes cause misaligned grids, unprofessional layouts,
and reduced customer trust. This document defines the image standards
for this store.

---

## Required Image Specifications

| Setting | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Width | 1440 pixels |
| Height | 1920 pixels |
| Aspect Ratio | 3:4 (portrait) |
| Format | JPG (photos), PNG (transparent backgrounds) |
| File Size | Under 1MB per image |
| Color Mode | RGB |
| Resolution | 72 DPI minimum |

---

## Why These Specifications

- **1440 x 1920px** is large enough for zoom features and retina 
  displays without being unnecessarily large
- **3:4 portrait ratio** matches the store's collection grid layout
  ensuring all product cards display at identical sizes
- **Under 1MB** keeps page load times fast which improves SEO 
  and customer experience
- **JPG format** provides the best balance of quality and file size 
  for jewelry photography

---

## Photography Guidelines

### Background
- Use a **clean white or light neutral background** for consistency
- Avoid busy or dark backgrounds unless part of brand aesthetic
- Ensure background is evenly lit with no shadows

### Lighting
- Use natural light or softbox lighting
- Avoid harsh direct flash which creates unwanted reflections on 
  metal and pearl surfaces
- Diffused lighting brings out the natural luster of pearls

### Composition
- Center the product in the frame
- Leave small equal margins on all sides
- For small items like earrings, use a close-up crop
- For bracelets and necklaces, show the full piece

### Multiple Images Per Product
- Recommended: 3-5 images per product
- Image 1: Clean product shot on white background (primary image)
- Image 2: Close-up detail shot showing texture and quality
- Image 3: Lifestyle shot showing the jewelry being worn
- Image 4: Flat lay showing scale and context
- Image 5: Packaging shot (optional)

## Image Preparation Tools

### Free Options
- **Canva** (canva.com) — resize and crop images online, free
- **Squoosh** (squoosh.app) — compress images without quality loss, free
- **Remove.bg** (remove.bg) — remove backgrounds automatically, free

### Paid Options
- **Adobe Lightroom** — professional photo editing
- **Photoshop** — advanced editing and background removal

---

## Resizing Workflow

If your photo is not 1440x1920px follow these steps in Canva:

1. Go to **canva.com** and create a free account
2. Click **Create a design → Custom size**
3. Enter **1440 x 1920 pixels**
4. Upload your product photo
5. Resize and center the product within the canvas
6. Add white background if needed
7. Download as **JPG** at maximum quality
8. Check file size is under 1MB before uploading to Shopify

---

## Uploading to Shopify

1. Go to **Products** in Shopify admin
2. Click the product you want to update
3. Click the existing image to replace it or drag new images in
4. Reorder images by dragging — first image is the primary display image
5. Click **Save**

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Mixing portrait and square images in the same store
- Uploading images larger than 5MB (slows page load significantly)
- Using screenshots from other websites (quality is too low)
- Inconsistent backgrounds across products
- Blurry or poorly lit images
- Images with visible watermarks

---

## Existing Images Needing Update

During the store setup, the following issues were identified:

- Several products had images smaller than 800x800px
- 5-6 products had non-standard aspect ratios causing grid misalignment
- These were manually recropped during setup as a temporary fix
- Recommend replacing these with properly photographed images at 
  the correct 1440x1920px standard
